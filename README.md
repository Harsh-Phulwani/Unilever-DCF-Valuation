# Unilever PLC (UNLVR.L) – DCF Valuation & Investment Analysis

This project presents a full Discounted Cash Flow (DCF) valuation of Unilever PLC (listed on the London Stock Exchange), built using Python and supported by detailed financial forecasts and an investment decision memo.

The objective is to estimate Unilever’s intrinsic value based on forward-looking fundamentals and assess whether the stock is undervalued or overvalued relative to its current market price.

---
## Project Overview

- Valuation Methodology: Free Cash Flow to Firm (FCFF) DCF
- Forecast Period: 5 years
- Terminal Value: Gordon Growth Method
- Currency: GBP
- Market: United Kingdom (LSE)

The analysis includes explicit forecasting of revenue, operating margins (EBIT), NOPAT, and FCFF, followed by WACC-based discounting to arrive at enterprise value and equity value.

---
## Key Valuation Steps

1. Historical financial analysis (Income Statement, Balance Sheet, Cash Flow)
2. Revenue growth and EBIT margin forecasting
3. NOPAT and FCFF estimation
4. Cost of Equity (CAPM) and Cost of Debt estimation
5. WACC calculation
6. Discounting forecasted FCFF and terminal value
7. Deriving enterprise value, equity value, and implied share price
8. Investment recommendation based on intrinsic value vs market price
---

## Key Outputs

- Intrinsic value per share: **~£61.5**
- Current market price at analysis date: **~£48**
- Implied upside: **~25–30%**
- Investment stance: **BUY** (under stated assumptions)
